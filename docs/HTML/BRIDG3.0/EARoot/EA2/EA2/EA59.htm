<p>Specifies the meaning (or semantics) of the relationship of one document to another.<br/></p><p><br/></p><p>For example, in a Regulated Product Submission (RPS), relationships of interest include "replaces" or "amends" (among others).  For example, support of versioning could be accomplished by having two different revisions of a document related through a "replaces" relationship. <br/></p>

Notes: A coded value specifying the kind of document relationship. Each value implies specific constraints to what kinds of objects can be related and in which way.<br /><br />For example, decomposition (component), pre-condition, post-condition, sequel (replaces, modifies), attribution (cause and effect) would be types of relationships that could be coded. Neutrophil count is a component of complete blood count. <br />The IV bag is weighed before and after the infusion. (sequel) <br />Injection site swelling and redness are attributed to the injection. (attribution)
