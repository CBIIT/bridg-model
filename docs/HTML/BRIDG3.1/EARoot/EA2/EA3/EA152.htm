<p>DEFINITION:<br/></p><p>One of a set of ordered partitions of a subject's, study subject's or experimental unit's participation in a study. An Epoch represents a state within a study such that subjects in separate arms within that state are comparable.<br/></p><p><br/></p><p>Each epoch serves a purpose in the study as a whole, typically exposing the subject to a treatment or preparing them for a treatment, or gathering post-treatment data.  Activities and activity results control the subject's movement from one epoch to another.<br/></p><p><br/></p><p>EXAMPLE(S):<br/></p><p>A study designed to assess the effects of treatments might have 3 epochs.<br/></p><p>A Screening Epoch in which subjects' eligibility is determined and baseline measurements are made.<br/></p><p>A Treatment Epoch during which treatments are given and effects of treatment are assessed.<br/></p><p>A Follow-up Epoch during which post-treatment assessments are conducted.  <br/></p><p><br/></p><p>OTHER NAME(S):<br/></p><p><br/></p><p>NOTE(S):<br/></p><p>A subject moves from one epoch to another and can only be in one epoch at a time. The subject can only move to an epoch with a greater sequenceNumber. Activities in the same epoch but a different arm need not be similar in time and pattern. Subjects in different arms will not necessarily pass through the same epochs.<br/></p>
