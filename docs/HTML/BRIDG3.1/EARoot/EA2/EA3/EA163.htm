<p>DEFINITION:<br/></p><p>A relationship between a composite activity and the component activities that comprise it, i.e. parent and child activities where all activities are intended to occur at some point in the context of a particular study.<br/></p><p><br/></p><p>EXAMPLE(S):<br/></p><p>A battery of tests may be composed of multiple routine labs that are always ordered together as a group. <br/></p><p><br/></p><p>A glucose tolerance test which is comprised of administering glucose and taking multiple timed blood samples which are then tested for glucose.<br/></p><p><br/></p><p>OTHER NAME(S):<br/></p><p><br/></p><p>NOTE(S):<br/></p><p>This class helps represent an AND relationship between siblings with the same parent activity.<br/></p>

Notes: DEFINITION:<br />A quantity of time that should elapse between when an activity is ready for execution and the actual beginning of the execution.<br /><br />EXAMPLE(S):<br />Take a blood sample 15 minutes after administration of study drug or measure blood glucose 2 hours after each meal, the pauseQuantity would be 15 minutes and 2 hours respectively. <br /><br />OTHER NAME(S):<br /><br />NOTE(S):<br />The pauseQuantity defines the amount of time between two points in time: (1) the point when all preceding steps (in sequence) and preconditions of an activity have been completed, and (2) the actual start of the activity. The completion of all pre-conditions and preceding steps (based on sequence number) indicates that an activity is "ready for execution" according to the definition above. The pauseQuantity counter or timeclock starts then, and when the full amount of time is reached, the activity begins. A pauseQuantity with a negative value means that the activity starts some amount of time prior to the estimated time that the activity is "ready for execution", i.e. prior to the estimated time that the pre-conditions and preceding steps will be completed.
