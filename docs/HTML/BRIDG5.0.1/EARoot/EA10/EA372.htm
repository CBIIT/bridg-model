DEFINITION:<br/>Specifies the link between an observation result and the actions taken as a result of that outcome.<br/><br/>EXAMPLE(S):<br/>An electrical problem (product investigation result) in a device could result in a recall (product action taken) of the device.<br/><br/>An abnormal lab result triggers a follow-up lab test for further investingation.<br/><br/>Study dose reduced or protocol treatment change due to an adverse event.<br/><br/>OTHER NAME(S):<br/>AdverseEventActionTakenRelationship, ProductActionTakenRelationship<br/><br/>NOTE(S): <br/>Action taken relationship is generally used for "unplanned" activities rather than those already determined in "contingent" relationships in the planned study design.<br/>
