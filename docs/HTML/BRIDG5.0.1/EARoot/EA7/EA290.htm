DEFINITION:<br/>One of a set of ordered partitions of a subject's participation in a study. An Epoch represents a state within a study such that subjects in separate arms within that state are comparable.<br/><br/>Each epoch serves a purpose in the study as a whole, typically exposing the subject to a treatment or preparing them for a treatment, or gathering post-treatment data.  Activities and activity results control the subject's movement from one epoch to another.<br/><br/>EXAMPLE(S):<br/>A study designed to assess the effects of treatments might have 3 epochs.<br/>A Screening Epoch in which subjects' eligibility is determined and baseline measurements are made.<br/>A Treatment Epoch during which treatments are given and effects of treatment are assessed.<br/>A Follow-up Epoch during which post-treatment assessments are conducted.  <br/><br/>OTHER NAME(S):<br/><br/>NOTE(S):<br/>A subject moves from one epoch to another and can only be in one epoch at a time. The subject can only move to an epoch with a greater sequenceNumber. Activities in the same epoch but a different arm need not be similar in time and pattern. Subjects in different arms will not necessarily pass through the same epochs.<br/>
